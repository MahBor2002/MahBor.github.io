Volunteering opportunity in Georgia

A little bit of backstory:
In May 12021 i got the offer to participate in a 1.5 month volunteering project in Tbilisi, Georgia. After a month of thinking and lots of stressful nights, i accepted the offer.
After all, this was probably a one in a lifetime chance and it was a great opportunity for me to learn living on my own and being independent.

Fast forward to July 2 nighttime and i landed in Tbilisi together with 5 other volunteers from Sweden.


A little bit about the project:
The youth organization DRONI together with the European Solidarity Corporation(ESC) have created project The sky is the limit. The project is about bringing young adults ranging in age from 18 to 30 to Georgia to help make positive difference. My role in the project is to raise awareness about our environment and teach locals to recycle. We have other volunteers from other european countries as well who have different tasks in the project. together we try to make the world a better place.



Update on day +10:
At the time I'm writing this, I've been in Georgia for a week and 3 days. Everything is going well and I'm getting used to this country. I've started to remember the routes and "live normally". We've started work in the office and things are going smoothly and nominally. Last tuesday we all went to a restaurant and got to try Georgian foods khinkali and khachapuri and they were pretty yummy! I've also been finding a lot of international friends here which is a big plus for me! Tomorrow our Italian roommate/volunteer leaves and our German volunteer leaves this saturday so that's a big bummer :(



Update on day +14:
This week has been the busiest week of my life! We had 2 events this week, one on wednesday and one on thursday. On wednesday we had a presentation about recycling in a youth center, and on thursday we had a Swedish cultural night. We spent most of the week preparing and planning the events and it was quite stressful.
Our German volunteer who has been really helpful and a good friend is leaving tomorrow so this afternoon has been a little sad for me. I will for sure miss Ghambashidze being busy and full of events.
I'll probably get my own room now that Chiara and Marlene are gone, but the apartment will be lonely with only Daniil, Marie, Djem and I.

Other than that everything is going really well with the Swedish crew and our projects. We've learned to work together and we have a great teamwork.
